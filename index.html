Welcome to Sintra

A fairytale town 30km from Lisbon, filled with palaces, castles, and stunning natural beauty

📍Location

25-40 minutes from Lisbon by train from Rossio station. Trains run every 20 minutes.

🏆UNESCO Site

Designated World Heritage Site since 1995 for its cultural landscape and Romanticist architecture.

🌳Nature

Home to Sintra-Cascais Nature Park and the scenic Sintra Mountains.

Must-See Attractions

Explore the most iconic palaces, castles, and sites in Sintra

🏰

Pena Palace

The crown jewel of Sintra — a colorful 19th-century Romanticist palace perched on a hilltop with breathtaking views.

⏱️ 1.5-2 hours | 💶 €14 (palace + park)
🌿

Quinta da Regaleira

Gothic estate with mysterious gardens, underground tunnels, and the famous spiral "Initiation Well".

⏱️ 2-3 hours | 💶 €10
⚔️

Moorish Castle

Medieval fortress ruins from the 8th-9th century offering panoramic views of the region.

⏱️ 1-1.5 hours | 💶 €8
👑

Sintra National Palace

Portuguese Renaissance palace in town center, famous for its distinctive twin conical chimneys.

⏱️ 1 hour | 💶 €10
🌺

Monserrate Palace

Exotic palace with stunning botanical gardens featuring plants from around the world.

⏱️ 1.5 hours | 💶 €8
🌊

Cabo da Roca

The westernmost point of mainland Europe — dramatic cliffs and ocean views (20 min from Sintra).

⏱️ 30-45 min | 💶 Free

One-Day Itinerary

Make the most of your Sintra visit with this optimized schedule

8:30 AM

🚂 Depart Lisbon

Take the train from Rossio station. Buy a round-trip ticket (€4.50 total). Arrive in Sintra around 9:10 AM.

9:30 AM

🏰 Pena Palace

Start early to beat the crowds! Take Bus 434 or a tuk-tuk. Explore the palace interior and walk the extensive gardens.

12:00 PM

⚔️ Moorish Castle (Optional)

Quick visit to the castle ruins if you have energy. Otherwise head back to town center.

1:00 PM

🍽️ Lunch in Historic Center

Try traditional Portuguese cuisine. Recommended: Tasca do Manel, Incomum, or Tascantiga.

2:30 PM

🌿 Quinta da Regaleira

Explore the mysterious gardens, climb down the Initiation Well, and discover hidden tunnels and grottos.

5:00 PM

☕ Coffee & Pastries

Try the famous travesseiros (almond pastries) at Piriquita before heading back to Lisbon.

6:00 PM

🚂 Return to Lisbon

Catch the train back from Sintra station. Trains run until midnight.

Practical Information

🚌Getting Around

  • Bus 434: Loops between train station, town center, and main palaces (€6.90 day pass)
  • Tuk-Tuk: €5-10/person for palace tours
  • Taxi/Uber: Available but can be pricey
  • Walking: Town center is walkable, but palaces are on steep hills

🎫Tickets & Combos

  • Buy tickets online in advance to skip lines
  • Combo tickets available for multiple sites
  • Parks of Sintra app for mobile tickets
  • Student/senior discounts available

🍴Where to Eat

  • Tasca do Manel (traditional Portuguese)
  • Incomum (modern Portuguese)
  • Piriquita (famous pastries since 1862)
  • Tascantiga (petiscos/tapas)

⏰Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (March-May): Mild weather, blooming gardens
  • Fall (September-November): Fewer crowds, pleasant temps
  • Arrive before 9 AM to beat tour groups
  • Avoid weekends if possible

Insider Tips

Make your Sintra experience unforgettable

👟 Wear Comfortable Shoes

Lots of walking on cobblestones and steep hills. Skip the heels!

🌤️ Bring Layers

Sintra can be 5-10°C cooler than Lisbon due to elevation and microclimates.

💧 Stay Hydrated

Bring a water bottle — climbing castle steps in summer heat is tiring!

📸 Go Early

Best photos and smallest crowds are before 10 AM at Pena Palace.

🎫 Book Online

Skip ticket lines by booking in advance on Parques de Sintra website.

🗺️ Pick Your Priorities

Don't try to see everything in one day. Focus on 2-3 main attractions.
